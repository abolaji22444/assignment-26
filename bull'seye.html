THE BULL'S EYE

The bullseye originated in the middle Ages,
with roots in archery practice, where shooters
aimed at a bull's skull to test skill and penetration. By
the 19th century, it evolved to desscribe the center
of a target, the thick center of spun crown-glass, or a
specialized lens for lanterns, symbolizing high precision.
Historically, archers aimed for the eye socket of a bull's
skull to demonstrate exceptional accuracy.
